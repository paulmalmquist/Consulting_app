## Post 2 — Washington Just Told You There Is No AI Regulator Coming. Now What?

**[Revenue purpose]** Credibility building
**[Target segment]** All segments; positions Paul as someone tracking policy that affects enterprise AI adoption
**[Confidence: HIGH on framework existence and key provisions]** — White House publication directly sourced

The White House released its National AI Policy Framework last week. Seven priority areas. Lots of language about innovation and competitiveness. But the most important thing in the document is what is not there.

No new federal AI regulatory body. No broad new compliance framework. Instead, the recommendation is sector-specific oversight through existing regulators and "regulatory sandboxes" for companies to experiment.

What this means for anyone building or buying AI for their business: the compliance environment is not going to get simpler anytime soon. There is no single rulebook coming. Instead you will have different rules for different industries, different states (unless federal preemption passes), and the EU adding its own layer in August.

For companies deploying AI in regulated industries like real estate finance, healthcare operations, or financial services, this creates a real operational question. How do you build AI systems that are transparent enough to satisfy whatever regulatory framework shows up, without waiting for the framework to be finalized before you start?

The answer is to build with transparency from the start. Log what your AI does and why. Make the reasoning auditable. Do not deploy black boxes that you will have to reverse-engineer later when a regulator asks how your system made a decision.

The firms building AI now with these principles will not have a compliance problem later. The firms building AI without them will.

[source: White House National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence (published March 20, 2026); Cooley LLP analysis — "White House Releases AI Regulatory Blueprint"; Holland & Knight — "White House Releases a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence"]
