---

## Top Gaps (Not in Winston, High Enterprise Value)

1. **Automated Operating Data Extraction from PDFs** — Easy-Moderate. The ability to upload a T-12, utility bill, or inspection report and have it automatically populate the asset's operating data record (rather than requiring manual entry) directly competes with what Cambio does. Winston already has document ingestion — extending it to structured extraction is the unlock.

2. **Capital Decision Approval Workflow** — Moderate. REPE asset managers are constantly seeking approval for capex, repairs, and improvements. A lightweight workflow (propose improvement → attach supporting analysis → route for GP approval → track status) embedded in Winston would be a meaningful operational add-on.

---

## Quick Wins (Already Partial — Just Need Assembly)

1. **Structured Extraction from Operating Documents** — Winston's document pipeline handles ingestion. Adding a structured extraction step that reads a T-12 or rent roll and populates the asset's financial data model (rather than just indexing it for search) is a high-ROI extension of existing infrastructure.

2. **Data Quality / Anomaly Flagging** — Winston aggregates GL data across assets. Adding a validation layer that flags when a reported number falls outside expected historical ranges (e.g., NOI drop >20% QoQ with no note) would surface data quality issues proactively — a direct competitor to Cambio's "4x data quality improvement" claim.
