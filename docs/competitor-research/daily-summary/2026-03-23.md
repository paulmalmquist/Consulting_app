## Yardi

### Findings
- **Yardi Virtuoso AI** is being positioned as the flagship 2026 product narrative — automating activities, making predictions, and surfacing next-best-action suggestions across the Yardi suite. Yardi is explicitly marketing data privacy (client data not used to retrain LLMs) as a differentiator.
- **Voyager 8** is shipping with smarter reporting, collections oversight improvements, and streamlined renewals for multifamily.
- **Hybrid Workplace Software** in development: new CEO Rob Teel is steering Yardi toward hybrid flex/coworking space management, building a software stack to connect employees to a global office network.
- **Maintenance IQ** received AI enhancements for multifamily maintenance diagnosis, documentation, and parts ordering.

### Winston Equivalent
- **AI automation / next-best-action:** Winston AI Gateway (189KB service, model dispatch, prompt policy) + MCP tool suite (31 categories). Winston's conversational AI layer is more flexible than Virtuoso's embedded automation — but Yardi's breadth of property management integration gives Virtuoso a stickier surface area.
- **Reporting:** Winston has REPE financial reporting, variance analysis, and PDF investor statements — but these are REPE-focused, not multifamily ERP.
- **Hybrid workplace:** No Winston equivalent. Out of scope for REPE focus.

### Threat Level: **MEDIUM**
Virtuoso AI narrative is maturing into a clear enterprise pitch. Yardi's data privacy positioning directly targets the same objection Winston will face. The Voyager 8 ERP upgrade deepens Yardi's moat in multifamily — less relevant for REPE/PE, but signals Yardi moving upmarket.

### Opportunity
Winston should explicitly counter Virtuoso's "embedded automation" pitch with a **workflow-native AI** story — Winston doesn't just automate property tasks, it reasons across the entire fund/deal/LP stack. Lean into the REPE-specific depth that Yardi's horizontal platform can't match. Add a data privacy statement to Winston's positioning docs.

## Juniper Square

### Findings
- **JunieAI** (launched with $130M Series D at $1.1B valuation, June 2025) is now in active deployment — described as "the first enterprise-grade AI built specifically for the needs of private markets GPs." Capital from Ribbit Capital + Fifth Wall.
- **Tenor Digital acquisition** (October 2025): Acquired for private credit capabilities. Tenor's AI ("Nia") uses OCR to process loan agreements and notices, automates post-close workflows, and delivers real-time calculations and portfolio analytics for private credit teams.
- Fund administration business: >100% 3-year CAGR, now managing 2,000+ fund entities globally. PE/VC now represent 4 of their 5 largest customers by revenue.
- Named "Investor Relations Technology of the Year" by Private Equity Wire US Awards 2025.

### Winston Equivalent
- **GP AI assistant:** Winston AI + chat workspace. JunieAI is narrowly scoped to GP workflows (IR, reporting, fund ops) — Winston's AI is broader and more configurable, but Juniper Square has deep fund admin integration Winston doesn't replicate.
- **Loan / private credit analytics:** Winston has Debt Surveillance (loan monitoring, rate sensitivity), but Tenor's Nia-powered OCR-to-workflow automation for private credit is more specialized.
- **Fund administration:** Winston has fund management (fund list, detail, metrics, waterfall engine) and LP management — but not third-party fund admin services at scale.
- **IR tech:** Winston has LP summary, waterfall, capital call management, investor statement PDF, IR draft assembly — competitive with Juniper Square's IR layer.

### Threat Level: **HIGH**
Juniper Square is now a $1.1B platform with dedicated GP AI, private credit capabilities via Tenor, and 2,000+ fund entities under administration. Their combination of fund admin services + JunieAI creates a bundled value proposition (software + outsourced ops + AI) that is difficult to unbundle. This is Winston's most direct competitive threat in the REPE/PE segment.

### Opportunity
Winston cannot win on fund admin services volume — but can win on **AI depth and configurability**. JunieAI is described as purpose-built for GPs; Winston should position its AI as purpose-built for the *entire deal-to-distribution lifecycle* — not just IR and fund reporting. Highlight Winston's scenario engine, Monte Carlo, and stress testing as capabilities JunieAI doesn't touch. Consider building a private credit OCR/workflow module to match Tenor's Nia capability (currently a gap).

- 2026 World Tour events kicking off across U.S. cities — marketing/awareness push.

### Winston Equivalent

- **Data validation:** Winston has integrity checks in portfolio analytics — but no dedicated data quality/observability surface.

### Threat Level: **LOW**
Cherre is a data infrastructure layer — it primarily competes on data connectivity and quality, not on AI analytics or deal workflow. The new observability UI is a feature improvement, not a strategic pivot. Limited overlap with Winston's REPE workflow layer.

### Opportunity
If Winston's clients are asking about data quality and pipeline visibility for their own upstream feeds, a lightweight **data health dashboard** (showing extraction confidence scores, pipeline status, validation flags) could be a differentiator. This would be an enhancement to the existing extraction engine — not a new build.

## Altus Group / ARGUS Intelligence

### Findings
- **ARGUS Intelligence March 2026 Release** (direct product release page confirmed): Extended scenario simulation modeling to **asset and portfolio levels** — investment teams can now test assumptions and assess risk exposure across entire portfolios in a single unified workflow. New capabilities: asset-level scenario creation, PDF/JPG export of simulation outputs, shareable vs. private simulation workflows.
- **Canadian Appraisals Business sold to Newmark** (closed ~March 1, 2026): Altus is divesting non-core appraisal services to sharpen focus on ARGUS Intelligence software. Newmark signed a multi-year ARGUS Intelligence license as part of the deal.
- Altus Group previously confirmed it is exploring a strategic sale — PE interest remains high.

### Winston Equivalent
- **Scenario simulation at asset + portfolio level:** Winston has a Scenario Engine (v1 + v2), stress testing (cap rate), sale scenarios, Monte Carlo simulation, and IRR timeline — this is strong parity. The key difference: ARGUS Intelligence is deeply integrated with CRE valuation workflows (DCF, ARGUS Enterprise models), while Winston's scenario engine is REPE-native.
- **PDF/JPG export of simulation outputs:** Winston generates investor statement PDFs — but simulation-specific export (PDF waterfall or scenario summary) may not be explicitly surfaced. Worth checking.
- **Portfolio-level scenario aggregation:** Winston has portfolio rollup views and geography analysis — the scenario engine should be verified for portfolio-level aggregation capability.

### Threat Level: **MEDIUM-HIGH**
ARGUS Intelligence's March release directly advances capabilities that overlap with Winston's scenario engine. The Newmark license deal also signals ARGUS is winning enterprise CRE relationships that could include REPE clients. The strategic sale process is noise for now — but a PE acquirer could inject capital and accelerate the ARGUS Intelligence roadmap significantly.

### Opportunity
Winston should document and market its **Monte Carlo + scenario engine** as a differentiator over ARGUS's deterministic scenario modeling. ARGUS Intelligence runs scenarios; Winston *simulates distributions of outcomes*. That's a meaningfully different capability. Also worth surfacing: Winston's scenario engine is embedded in a full deal-to-LP workflow — ARGUS remains primarily a valuation/analysis tool without the fund/LP/IR layer.
