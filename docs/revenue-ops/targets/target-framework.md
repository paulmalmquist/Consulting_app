# Target Account Framework

## Segment Definitions

### Segment 1: South Florida Mid-Market Businesses
- **Revenue range:** $10M-$500M
- **Employee count:** 50-500
- **Why fit:** Geographic proximity for in-person, mid-market sweet spot where AI adoption is lowest but ROI highest
- **Matching offers:** AI Operations Diagnostic (Offer A), AI Fluency Workshop (Offer C)
- **Proof needed:** Local references, workflow transformation examples, ROI calculator
- **Channels:** Local events, BNI/chambers, LinkedIn geo+title targeting, warm intros
- **Signals:** Recently hired Director of Ops or VP Tech, mentioned AI in updates, rapid headcount growth, manual reporting complaints

### Segment 2: REPE / Private Equity Operators ($500M-$5B AUM)
- **Why fit:** Acute data fragmentation, fundraising pressure, willingness to pay for infrastructure
- **Matching offers:** Portfolio Intelligence Audit (Offer B), Winston Platform Pilot
- **Proof needed:** Winston REPE demo, industry language fluency, LP reporting examples
- **Channels:** NMHC/IMN/CREFC conferences, LinkedIn to CFOs/Heads of Portfolio Ops, industry newsletters
- **Signals:** Recent fund close, new data/ops hire, Yardi renewal timing, LP reporting complaints, 3+ data sources

### Segment 3: Real Estate Developers / Construction (PDS)
- **Why fit:** Active construction = immediate quantifiable pain, multi-party coordination burden
- **Matching offers:** Project Delivery Intelligence Setup
- **Proof needed:** PDS demo, Procore integration proof, sample OAC automation
- **Channels:** ENR rankings for South FL GCs, developer events, Procore user community, LinkedIn by project role
- **Signals:** Active $50M+ projects, multiple job sites, Procore/ACC adoption, compliance complexity (LEED, Passive House)

### Segment 4: Legal / Healthcare Ops
- **Why fit:** Document-heavy, compliance-driven, high manual overhead, obvious AI ROI but slow adoption
- **Matching offers:** AI Operations Diagnostic (Offer A), AI Fluency Workshop (Offer C)
- **Proof needed:** HIPAA/compliance awareness, document workflow examples, risk-mitigation framing
- **Channels:** Local bar association, healthcare ops conferences, LinkedIn, referral network
- **Signals:** Expanding ops team, compliance audit failures, leadership change, tech modernization budget

### Segment 5: Workshop Attendees (Conversion Funnel)
- **Why fit:** Self-selected, already interested, lowest friction conversion
- **Matching offers:** Workshop → Diagnostic → Implementation
- **Proof needed:** Workshop quality, follow-up diagnostic offer, ROI examples
- **Channels:** Attendee list, post-event email, 1:1 follow-ups, LinkedIn connection
- **Signals:** Stayed for Q&A, asked business-specific questions, requested follow-up, connected on LinkedIn same day

## Qualification Criteria (BANT+)

A lead is "qualified" when we have confirmed:
- **Budget:** They have or can access budget for this type of engagement ($5K+ for diagnostic, $25K+ for audit)
- **Authority:** We're talking to someone who can sign or directly influence the decision
- **Need:** They've described a specific pain that maps to one of our offers
- **Timeline:** They have a reason to act within 60 days (not "someday")
- **Fit:** Their org size, complexity, and industry match our offer architecture

Score in CRM: lead_score >= 50 means qualified. Components:
- Pain clarity (0-25): Vague awareness = 5, described specific problem = 15, quantified cost = 25
- Budget signal (0-25): No signal = 0, mentioned budget = 10, confirmed range = 20, approved = 25
- Authority (0-20): End user = 5, influencer = 10, decision maker = 15, champion + decision maker = 20
- Timeline (0-20): No urgency = 0, "this quarter" = 10, "this month" = 15, "this week/active eval" = 20
- Fit (0-10): Partial match = 3, strong match = 7, ideal profile = 10
