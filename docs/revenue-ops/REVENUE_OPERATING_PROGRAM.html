Revenue-Backwards Operating Framework

⌄

Seven-stage pipeline working backwards from cash collected to initial prospecting. Each stage has clear entry/exit criteria, ownership, and evidence requirements.

Stage Objective Entry Criteria Exit Criteria Owner Evidence Blockers
Revenue Received Cash collected Invoice paid N/A Paul Bank deposit, marked paid Payment delays, disputes
Proposal Accepted Signed SOW Proposal sent + follow-up Signed document Paul Signed SOW or email acceptance Price objection, scope fear, momentum loss
Proposal Sent Formal offer delivered Discovery complete, scope agreed Proposal opened + follow-up scheduled Paul Delivery confirmation, calendar hold Slow drafting, unclear scope, pricing uncertainty
Discovery Call Held Understand pain, budget, timeline, authority Qualified lead confirmed, meeting scheduled Pain confirmed, budget known, next step agreed Paul Meeting notes, pain point confirmed, budget signal No-show, vague pain, no budget authority
Qualified Lead Confirm fit and intent Target identified, initial response Pain + budget + authority + timeline (BANT) Paul Response to outreach, interest, pain signal False positives, low urgency, wrong contact
Target Account Selected Identify high-probability accounts Segment defined, signals scanned Account researched, contact identified, outreach drafted Paul Account brief, contact info, draft ready Insufficient research, wrong segment, no path
Outreach Initiated Start conversations Target list built, assets ready, templates drafted First touch sent (email, LinkedIn, referral) Paul Outreach log, template, channel recorded No proof assets, weak messaging, wrong channel

Offer Architecture

⌄

Three packaged offers designed for different buyer maturity and budget levels. All three funnel upward to SaaS platform.

Offer A: AI Operations Diagnostic
Value Prop
"In two weeks, we'll map exactly where AI saves your team the most time and money, and implement your first quick win before we leave."
Scope
5–10 workflow audit, AI opportunity mapping, ROI scoring, one quick-win implementation
Deliverables
Diagnostic report, prioritized AI roadmap, one implemented automation, executive presentation
Timeline
2 weeks
Pricing
$7,500 (diagnostic only) / $15,000 (+ first implementation)
Success Looks Like
Client has clear AI roadmap + at least one automation saving 5+ hrs/week
Offer B: Portfolio Intelligence Audit
For
REPE firms ($500M–$5B AUM) with 3+ data sources and LP reporting pressure
Scope
Data source inventory, reporting gap analysis, LP transparency scorecard, unified dashboard prototype
Timeline
3–4 weeks
Pricing
$25,000 (audit) / $75,000 (audit + prototype implementation)
Success Looks Like
Client sees data gaps quantified + has working prototype of unified reporting
Offer C: AI Fluency Workshop
For
Executive teams, department heads, business owners needing informed AI decisions
Scope
Half-day or full-day workshop with live demos, exercises, customized industry examples
Timeline
1 day (workshop) + 2-week follow-up
Pricing
$3,500 (half-day) / $6,000 (full-day) / $10,000 (full-day + post-workshop diagnostic)
Success Looks Like
Attendees have actionable AI plan; 30–50% conversion to diagnostic or consulting

Target Account Segments

⌄

Five customer segments with matching offers, proof requirements, and outreach channels.

Segment 1: South Florida Mid-Market Businesses
Why Fit
Geographic proximity, mid-market sweet spot ($10M–$500M revenue), lowest AI adoption but highest ROI
Matching Offer
AI Operations Diagnostic, AI Fluency Workshop
Reach Strategy
Local business events, BNI/chambers, LinkedIn geo-targeting + titles, warm intros
Promising Signals
Recently hired VP of Tech, mentioned AI in updates, expanding headcount rapidly
Segment 2: REPE / Private Equity ($500M–$5B AUM)
Why Fit
Acute data fragmentation, fundraising pressure for LP reporting, willingness to pay for infrastructure
Matching Offer
Portfolio Intelligence Audit, Winston Platform Pilot
Reach Strategy
Industry conferences (NMHC, IMN, CREFC), LinkedIn CFO/Portfolio Ops targeting, newsletter sponsorship
Promising Signals
Recent fund close, new hire in data/ops, Yardi renewal timing, LP reporting complaints
Segment 3: Real Estate Developers / Construction (PDS)
Why Fit
Construction projects have immediate, quantifiable pain around coordination and OAC reporting
Matching Offer
Project Delivery Intelligence Setup
Reach Strategy
ENR rankings for South Florida GCs, developer associations, Procore community, LinkedIn project roles
Promising Signals
Active $50M+ projects, multiple job sites, recent Procore adoption, compliance complexity
Segment 4: Legal / Healthcare Ops Firms
Why Fit
Document-heavy, compliance-driven workflows with high manual overhead; obvious ROI but slow adoption
Matching Offer
AI Operations Diagnostic, AI Fluency Workshop
Reach Strategy
Bar association events, healthcare ops conferences, LinkedIn compliance+tech targeting
Promising Signals
Expanding ops team, compliance audit failures, leadership change, modernization budget
Segment 5: Workshop Attendees → Consulting Conversion
Why Fit
Self-selected, already interested, saw Paul present; lowest friction conversion path
Matching Offer
AI Fluency Workshop → AI Operations Diagnostic → Implementation
Reach Strategy
Post-event email sequence, 1:1 follow-up calls, LinkedIn connections, referral ask
Promising Signals
Stayed for Q&A, asked specific questions, requested follow-up, connected on LinkedIn same day
