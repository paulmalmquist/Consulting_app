## Regime Context

The RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE regime is paradoxically favorable for defense and space equities. While most sectors face headwinds from geopolitical uncertainty and tightening financial conditions, defense is the direct beneficiary of the Iran war and the broader global rearmament trend. The S&P 500 Aerospace and Defense Index has gained 53.5 percent over the past year, dramatically outperforming the broader market. The regime boosts positioning risk and volatility regime signals, but for defense, elevated positioning risk actually reflects crowded-but-justified positioning into a sector with structural demand tailwinds. The 0.8x regime multiplier applies across categories, but the raw signal strength in defense is high enough to produce a strong composite even after the discount.

## Signal Scores

**Price Momentum: 8 out of 10.** Defense and space stocks are in a powerful uptrend supported by fundamental catalysts rather than speculative excess. The S&P Aerospace and Defense Index gained 53.5 percent over the past year. In early March 2026, Lockheed Martin advanced more than 3 percent in a single session after surging 6.9 percent in premarket, while Northrop Grumman climbed 5.8 percent in early premarket trading. RTX climbed 4 percent as investors rotated into defense names following the escalation in Iran. Firefly Aerospace shares are up 34 percent year-to-date and remain a high-beta favorite in the space subsector. AeroVironment reported revenue growth exceeding 150 percent year-over-year in its Q2 2026 earnings. The sector is outperforming on both absolute and relative bases, with new highs being set on high volume.

**Fundamental Quality: 8 out of 10.** The fundamental case for defense is among the strongest in the equity market. President Trump has proposed a 13.4 percent increase in the fiscal 2026 defense budget to 1.01 trillion dollars, providing a massive government-backed demand floor. RTX issued fiscal year 2026 earnings guidance of 6.60 to 6.80 dollars per share, well above the analyst consensus of roughly 6.11 dollars, a positive surprise that reflects strong backlog execution. Northrop Grumman generated 41.95 billion dollars in 2025 revenue. Order backlogs are robust across all major primes, with Raytheon securing new agreements for increased production of Tomahawk cruise missiles, AMRAAM air-to-air missiles, and SM-6 interceptors. The 613-billion-dollar global space economy provides additional growth vectors through satellite, launch, and defense-space crossover programs.

**Positioning Risk: 6 out of 10 (regime-boosted).** The defense sector has attracted significant capital inflows, creating some positioning risk from crowded trades. The 53.5 percent one-year return means many investors have substantial unrealized gains that could be taken during any sell-the-news event. However, the structural demand story (Iran war, 1-trillion-dollar defense budget, global rearmament) provides a fundamental floor that distinguishes this crowding from speculative positioning. The regime boost to this signal is appropriate given that defense is one of the few sectors where positioning risk is elevated. Major valuations reflect this: RTX market cap at 256.7 billion, Lockheed Martin at 144.4 billion, Northrop Grumman at 97.8 billion.

**Catalyst Density: 8 out of 10 (regime-dampened, raw score 9).** The catalyst calendar for defense and space is exceptionally dense. The Iran war is the immediate catalyst, driving both direct procurement acceleration and a broader shift in defense spending priorities toward AI-enabled platforms, cyber capabilities, space systems, and missile defense. The Golden Dome initiative has created a new procurement vehicle that benefits companies like Firefly Aerospace (whose SciTec acquisition enhances missile detection capabilities), AeroVironment (combat-proven Switchblade and Puma systems), Palantir (AI and data fusion for defense), and Redwire (whose stock rocketed 29 percent after joining the 151-billion-dollar Golden Dome contract). Boeing's 737 MAX production ramp to 38 per month in Q1 2026 provides a commercial aerospace catalyst alongside the defense story.

**Volatility Regime: 5 out of 10.** Defense stocks exhibit lower implied volatility than the broader market during the current risk-off environment, which is unusual and reflects the sector's safe-haven status. While VIX is at 25.33 for the S&P 500, defense names are trading with relatively compressed vol, meaning options-based hedging and entry strategies are relatively inexpensive for this sector. The space subsector is more volatile, with FLY and other small-cap space names showing elevated beta.

**Cross-Vertical Relevance: 5 out of 10.** Defense and space has moderate cross-vertical relevance. For REPE, the defense spending boom drives demand for industrial and logistics real estate near major defense installations and manufacturing facilities. Data center demand from defense AI applications connects to the broader AI infrastructure real estate theme. For PDS, defense construction and base expansion projects create demand for project delivery services. The connection to Credit is weaker, limited mainly to the health of defense contractor supply chains and their financing needs.

## Key Findings

**Finding 1: Trillion-Dollar Defense Budget.** President Trump's proposed fiscal 2026 defense budget of 1.01 trillion dollars, representing a 13.4 percent increase, establishes a spending floor that is unprecedented in nominal terms. Defense priorities are shifting toward AI-enabled platforms, cyber and space capabilities, and missile defense, which benefits technology-forward contractors over traditional hardware-only firms. This budget proposal creates a multi-year visibility into demand that few other sectors can match. Source: deloitte.com

**Finding 2: AeroVironment Revenue Growth Exceeds 150 Percent.** AeroVironment's Q2 2026 earnings showed revenue growth exceeding 150 percent year-over-year, making it one of the fastest-growing defense companies in the market. The company's combat-proven Switchblade 600 and Puma AE systems, combined with its BlueHalo acquisition expanding into counter-UAS, space, cyber, and electronic warfare, position it at the intersection of every defense spending priority. KeyBanc named it a highest-conviction idea with industry-leading margins. Source: finance.yahoo.com

**Finding 3: Golden Dome Creates New Procurement Catalyst.** The Golden Dome missile defense initiative has already driven dramatic stock moves, including Redwire's 29 percent single-day surge after joining the 151-billion-dollar contract. Firefly Aerospace's SciTec acquisition enhances its missile detection and analytics capabilities specifically for Golden Dome and FORGE programs. This initiative represents a new, incremental demand driver on top of existing defense budgets. Source: cnbc.com

**Finding 4: Space Economy Reaches 613 Billion Dollars.** The global space economy reached 613 billion dollars in 2024 with commercial contributions accounting for 78 percent of total growth. The Space Development Agency's low-Earth-orbit constellation programs and the consolidation around vertically integrated satellite-and-launch platforms are creating M&A opportunities and organic growth vectors for publicly traded space companies. Deal flow in the space sector is increasingly focused on reducing capital intensity through partnerships. Source: pwc.com, startus-insights.com

**Finding 5: Iran War Accelerating Procurement Timelines.** The ongoing war in Iran is not just a sentiment catalyst but is actively accelerating procurement timelines. Raytheon has secured agreements to boost production of Tomahawk, AMRAAM, and SM-6 missiles. The conflict is demonstrating the real-world demand for the exact capabilities that defense budgets are prioritizing, creating a positive feedback loop between wartime consumption and future procurement authorization. Source: fool.com
