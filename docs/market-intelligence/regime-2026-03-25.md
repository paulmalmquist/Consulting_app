# Market Regime Report — March 25, 2026

**Classification: RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE**
**Confidence: High**
**Prior Regime: RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE (no change)**

---

## Current Regime

The market regime remains RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE for a second consecutive session, with the weight of evidence continuing to favor caution across risk assets. No regime change has occurred since the transition logged on March twenty-second, but the signals have incrementally deteriorated, confirming rather than reversing the defensive posture.

## Signal Readings

**S&P 500 Momentum.** The S&P five hundred is trading near sixty-six hundred, having retreated roughly five to six percent from its seven thousand peak set earlier in twenty twenty-six. The index is now testing its two-hundred-day moving average — the critical technical boundary that has defined the post-twenty twenty-four bull market trend — rather than sitting comfortably above it. The fifty-day moving average has also turned lower and is converging toward the two-hundred-day, a pattern historically associated with trending deterioration rather than a pause within an uptrend. This is a bearish signal.

**VIX and Term Structure.** The CBOE Volatility Index closed near twenty-seven on March twenty-fourth, its highest sustained level in over a year. "Extreme Fear" characterizes current sentiment readings. At twenty-seven, the VIX is meaningfully elevated — not yet in the panic zone above thirty-five that would point to RISK_OFF_PANIC, but well above the sub-fifteen readings that define low-volatility risk-on environments. The primary drivers are geopolitical: the US-Israel military operation against Iran that began in late February has injected persistent macro uncertainty into the tape.

**High Yield Credit Spreads.** The ICE BofA US High Yield Option-Adjusted Spread is tracking at approximately three hundred and twenty basis points — widening from the tighter levels seen in late twenty twenty-five. While three-twenty is not a distress reading by historical standards, the directional trend matters: spreads are moving against risk assets, not compressing. This corroborates the equity signal.

**Bitcoin-SPX Correlation.** In an unusual development, Bitcoin is diverging from equities. BTC is holding the seventy thousand dollar psychological support level while the S&P five hundred has declined, and the twenty-day correlation has turned modestly negative near negative zero-point-five. This decoupling — with Bitcoin beginning to behave more like digital gold than a high-beta tech proxy — is consistent with macro stress, where hard-asset alternatives attract flows. The divergence does not change the overall regime but adds texture: this is a macro-driven risk-off, not a broad indiscriminate liquidation.

**US Dollar (DXY).** The dollar index is trading near ninety-nine-point-three, having rebounded from a February low near ninety-six but failing to hold the one-oh-one-plus levels briefly tested in mid-March. The dollar is in a strengthening trend on a one-month basis, up roughly one-point-six percent, which is consistent with risk-off dollar demand and geopolitical safe-haven flows. Dollar strength typically compresses returns on international and commodity-sensitive equity exposures.

**M2 Money Supply.** US M2 reached a record twenty-two-point-four-four trillion dollars, growing four-point-three percent year-over-year — the twenty-second consecutive monthly increase. This modest but consistent liquidity expansion is a mild structural tailwind for asset prices and reduces the probability of a credit-driven systemic event. It is the one constructive signal in an otherwise defensive signal set.

## Regime Confidence: High

Five of six signals point to defensive conditions. VIX is elevated, SPX is testing its long-term trend line, credit spreads are widening, and the dollar is bid. The only bullish offset is the sustained M2 expansion. Confidence in the RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE classification is high. The regime would upgrade to TRANSITION_UP if VIX retreated below twenty, SPX reclaimed and held its two-hundred-day moving average, and spreads began tightening. A deterioration toward RISK_OFF_PANIC would require VIX to spike above thirty-five with correlations across all asset classes moving sharply toward one.

## Implications by Asset Category

Equities should be sized conservatively, with a preference for defensive sectors — utilities, consumer staples, and healthcare — over high-beta technology and speculative growth. Fixed income duration provides ballast in a risk-off environment. Commodities face a crosscurrent: geopolitical oil supply disruption argues for energy longs, but dollar strength is a headwind to metals outside of gold. Digital assets are experiencing a rare decoupling, with Bitcoin acting as a macro hedge rather than a risk asset, which warrants small allocation consideration in diversified portfolios. Cash and short-duration instruments remain attractive given the elevated uncertainty premium in the tape.
