## Key Findings

First, the inference inflection is the single most important structural theme. NVIDIA's Dynamo OS achieving seven times throughput improvement on Blackwell GPUs signals that the next compute wave is inference at scale — not model training. This expands the total addressable market beyond frontier labs to every enterprise deploying production AI workloads. The market for inference chips is potentially larger and more recurring than training compute.

Second, hyperscaler capex commitment is unusual in its visibility. Over one point one trillion dollars in combined AI infrastructure orders across just three hyperscalers creates a demand floor that is unprecedented in semiconductor history. The risk is not demand — it is execution, yield, and supply chain.

Third, the China HBM wildcard is material. CXMT is targeting domestic HBM3 production by end of two thousand twenty-six, using stockpiled ASML DUV equipment. If Chinese producers achieve even moderate yield on HBM3, it partially validates a supply chain that bypasses US export controls. This is a long-term negative for SK Hynix and Micron's premium positioning on HBM.

Fourth, the ASML EUV choke point remains decisive. China's inability to access EUV lithography creates a manufacturing floor that limits leading-edge fabrication. ASML's EUV monopoly is the deepest competitive moat in the entire semiconductor supply chain — more durable than any software advantage.

Fifth, in the current RISK_OFF regime, semiconductor stocks face multiple compression risk even if earnings estimates hold. P/E multiples on AI hardware names were pricing near-perfection; any guide-down or demand softening triggers outsized downside. The margin of safety is thin at current valuations.
