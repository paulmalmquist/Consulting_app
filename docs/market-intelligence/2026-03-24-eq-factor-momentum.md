## Regime Context

The RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE regime is the most adversarial possible environment for a momentum factor screen. The regime shift triggers two compounding risks simultaneously: the underlying portfolio rebalances away from prior momentum winners (technology, AI names), and the factor itself historically crashes hardest during exactly these transition moments — when prior winners roll over and mean reversion accelerates. The zero point eight composite multiplier applies, but the more important regime signal is qualitative: momentum crash risk is at its highest when a strong prior trend reverses sharply, which is precisely what the SPX technical breakdown represents.

## Signal Scores (Pre-Regime Adjustment)

**Price Momentum: 4 out of 10.** The current market rotation is a meaningful headwind for the trailing twelve-to-one-month momentum signal. Technology, AI, and semiconductors — the dominant prior momentum leaders — have broken below their fifty and two hundred day moving averages. Industrial stocks have surged over sixteen percent year-to-date in two thousand twenty-six, with Caterpillar up thirty-two percent. Energy stocks are up over twenty-two percent. Consumer defensives up thirteen percent. This means the momentum portfolio is undergoing a painful sector-level reshuffling: prior winners (Mag Seven proxies) are now losers, and prior laggards (energy, industrials) are now winners. The twelve-to-one lookback period is capturing this reversal in real time.

**Fundamental Quality: 6 out of 10.** The new momentum leaders — energy majors like Exxon and Chevron, industrial names like Caterpillar, and consumer staples like Walmart and Costco — have better-than-average fundamental quality. They are not overvalued relative to historical norms. The S&P five hundred's forward equity risk premium is near an all-time low at zero point zero two percent above the ten-year Treasury, suggesting the broad index is overvalued, but the new momentum leaders in defensives and energy trade at significantly more reasonable multiples.

**Positioning Risk: 8 out of 10** (boosted under RISK_OFF). This is the most critical signal for this segment. Research shows factor crowding in momentum has been reaching new extremes in two thousand twenty-six, with record concentration and a winner-takes-all dynamic. Hedge fund exposure to crowded momentum books amplifies tail risk precisely in moments of market stress. Momentum portfolio rebalancing explains one to two percent of total order flow, and this percentage has been growing. When crowded momentum names unwind, the selling is mechanical and independent of fundamentals. The current environment checks all three historical momentum crash predictors: prior strong momentum performance, elevated valuations, and now a rising rate and falling equity environment.

**Catalyst Density: 5 out of 10** (dampened under RISK_OFF). The primary catalyst for the momentum factor in the near term is the completion of sector rotation — identifying which new names will occupy the top momentum decile going forward. Industrial and energy stocks carry their own earnings catalysts (infrastructure spend, energy prices, government contract flows). However, the dampening under RISK_OFF is appropriate because catalyst-driven momentum is harder to sustain when the macro backdrop is deteriorating. Small caps are outpacing large caps in an early reversal that adds complexity to screen construction.

**Volatility Regime: 4 out of 10** (boosted under RISK_OFF). VIX at twenty-six to twenty-seven with the SPX in a four-week losing streak is the worst possible setup for running levered momentum exposure. The factor performs best in sustained low-volatility trending environments. Elevated volatility increases whipsaw risk and expands bid-ask spreads on momentum rebalancing trades. For a pure momentum screen, this regime is a red flag.

**Cross-Vertical Relevance: 5 out of 10.** Momentum factor shifts act as leading indicators for sector capital allocation. The current rotation into industrials and energy is directly relevant to PDS (infrastructure project pipeline) and REPE (industrial and energy real estate demand). Tracking which sectors are entering the top momentum decile provides forward signal for physical asset demand roughly six to twelve months out.

## Key Findings

First, the sector rotation underway in two thousand twenty-six is one of the sharpest in recent memory. Industrials, energy, and consumer defensives are leading the S&P five hundred higher while technology names — which dominated momentum screens throughout two thousand twenty-three through twenty twenty-five — are correcting. This represents a major recomposition of the top-decile momentum portfolio.

Second, momentum crash conditions are present and elevated. Academic research on momentum crashes identifies three predictors: when momentum recently performed very well (true — the prior RISK_ON_MOMENTUM regime delivered strong returns), when interest rates are relatively low or rising (rates are elevated and policy is uncertain), and when the broader market breaks down (true — four consecutive weeks of losses, SPX below two hundred day moving average). All three conditions are met simultaneously, making this one of the higher-risk periods for a long-only momentum strategy.

Third, the factor crowding problem is structural, not cyclical. Style positioning in two thousand twenty-six resembles two thousand twenty-five with new extremes. Record concentration and a winner-takes-all dynamic means any unwind is amplified. The empirical relationship between crowding and crash risk has grown stronger as factor AUM has scaled.

Fourth, cross-asset momentum reads are mixed but informative. Gold and metals have been top performers — gold is up significantly, reflecting safe-haven demand. Energy is performing well on supply discipline and structural demand. FX momentum favors the USD. Commodity momentum is net positive. This cross-asset picture suggests momentum as a factor is alive, but the equity-specific implementation needs to rotate its sector weights aggressively.

Fifth, the small-cap momentum opportunity deserves attention. Small caps outpacing large caps in an early reversal pattern is historically associated with recoveries or broad broadening moves. If this continues, small-cap momentum screens may outperform large-cap implementations in the next thirty to sixty days.

## Feature Gaps Identified

No automated top-decile momentum screen exists in the current environment — manual construction from price return data is required. Cross-asset momentum composite (equities plus FX plus commodities plus bonds) is not yet built and would meaningfully improve the signal quality of this segment. A factor crowding monitor — tracking hedge fund positioning concentration across factor exposures — is not available without prime brokerage data access. Historical momentum crash episode database for backtesting under comparable regimes does not exist in the current system.
