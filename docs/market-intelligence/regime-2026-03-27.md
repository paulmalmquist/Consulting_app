# Market Regime Report — March 27, 2026

## Classification: RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE

Confidence: High

No regime change from the prior assessment on March 24. The defensive posture remains firmly in place as equity markets trade below key technical levels, volatility stays elevated, and the dollar strengthens on geopolitical tailwinds.

## Signal Summary

The S&P 500 closed around 5,592, sitting below both its 50-day moving average at roughly 5,615 and its 200-day moving average near 5,769. That is a clear bearish technical posture. The index hit its lowest close of 2026 earlier this month and has failed to reclaim either moving average since late February.

The VIX printed at 25.33, well above the long-run average, while the three-month VIX (VIX3M) came in at 23.93. That puts the term structure in backwardation, meaning near-term fear exceeds longer-dated implied volatility. Backwardation at these levels typically signals that hedging demand is concentrated on immediate downside risk rather than a slow grind lower.

High yield credit spreads on the ICE BofA US High Yield Index registered 3.19 percent, essentially unchanged from the 3.20 percent reading at the last regime change. Spreads are not blowing out, which argues against an upgrade to RISK_OFF_PANIC, but they remain wider than the sub-three-percent levels that characterized the risk-on environment earlier in the year. The BB tranche sits at 1.90 percent and Single-B at 3.49 percent, showing modest stress in lower-quality credits.

Bitcoin and the S&P 500 are moving in lockstep again, with the 30-day correlation coefficient climbing to 0.74, the highest reading of 2026. When crypto and equities correlate this tightly it usually means macro forces — rates, liquidity, geopolitics — are dominating all risk assets simultaneously. Earlier in March there was a brief decorrelation episode, but it has fully reversed.

The DXY dollar index rose to 99.90 on Thursday, its third straight session of gains. The greenback has bounced sharply from its February low near 96, driven by Middle East disruptions pushing energy prices higher and reinforcing expectations the Fed will hold rates steady through the year. A strengthening dollar in a risk-off regime adds pressure to emerging markets and commodities, reinforcing the defensive tone.

M2 money supply grew 4.9 percent year over year through February 2026, a modest uptick from the 4.2 percent pace in December. Money supply is expanding but not at a rate that would overpower the current headwinds. Liquidity is supportive on a longer horizon but not sufficient to flip the regime by itself.

## Change from Prior Regime

No change. The current regime has been RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE since March 22 when the S&P 500 broke below its 200-day moving average after four consecutive weekly losses.

## Implications by Asset Category

Equities remain vulnerable to further downside or range compression until the S&P reclaims the 50-day moving average. Defensive sectors like utilities, healthcare, and consumer staples are favored over cyclicals and high-beta tech. Earnings quality and balance sheet strength matter more in this environment than growth multiples.

Fixed income benefits from the flight to safety, though the Fed holding steady limits duration upside. Short-duration investment-grade credit and Treasuries are the cleaner expressions of the defensive theme. High yield needs spread stabilization before being attractive on a risk-adjusted basis.

Crypto is trading as a macro risk asset in this regime, not a hedge. The high correlation to SPX means Bitcoin and Ethereum will likely continue to track equity sentiment rather than diverge. Positioning should be lighter until the correlation breaks.

Commodities are mixed. Energy benefits from geopolitical supply disruption, but base metals and industrial commodities are pressured by the stronger dollar and growth concerns. Gold is constructive as a safe haven but faces headwinds from the dollar rally.

Foreign exchange positioning favors the dollar in the near term given rate differentials and safe-haven flows. Emerging market currencies are under pressure from the DXY strength and risk-off sentiment.
