# Market Regime Report for March 26, 2026

## Current Regime: RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE

Confidence: Moderate

The market remains in a defensive risk-off posture for the fifth consecutive session, though conditions are showing early signs of stabilization. The S and P 500 closed at approximately six thousand five hundred six, still trading below both its fifty-day moving average near six thousand eight hundred eighteen and its two hundred-day moving average around six thousand five hundred ninety-two. This is the first extended period since May 2025 where the index has sat below both trend lines simultaneously, confirming the intermediate-term downtrend remains intact.

Volatility has improved meaningfully since the March 22 regime change. The VIX declined to twenty-two, down from nearly twenty-seven when the defensive regime was first triggered. The VIX three-month index sits at roughly twenty-four, maintaining a normal contango term structure where longer-dated volatility exceeds near-term implied volatility. This contango shape suggests the market is not pricing in an imminent crash, even as uncertainty remains elevated. The VIX at twenty-two is above its long-run median but well below panic thresholds near thirty or higher.

High yield credit spreads remain essentially unchanged at three point one nine percent on the ICE BofA US High Yield Index, compared to three point two zero percent when the regime was first classified. BB-rated paper trades at one point nine zero percent and single-B at three point four nine percent. Spreads are wider than the sub-two-fifty levels seen during the 2025 risk-on stretch but have not accelerated, which argues against an imminent credit stress event.

The Bitcoin to S and P 500 thirty-day correlation has climbed to zero point seven four, the highest reading of 2026. Elevated cross-asset correlation typically signals a macro-driven market where diversification benefits shrink and risk assets move in lockstep. Some analysts have noted brief decoupling episodes tied to geopolitical catalysts, but the dominant regime is one of correlated selling.

The dollar index sits at ninety-nine point six, essentially flat over the past week and down roughly four point seven percent over the trailing twelve months. The weaker dollar trend has been a modest tailwind for commodities and international earnings but has not been enough to offset equity weakness. M2 money supply continues to expand at roughly four point nine percent year over year, a moderate pace that supports liquidity conditions without signaling either aggressive monetary easing or tightening.

## Change from Prior Regime

No change. The regime has been RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE since March 22, when the S and P 500 broke below its two hundred-day moving average amid four consecutive weekly losses and a VIX spike to nearly twenty-seven. The key difference today is that volatility has receded meaningfully, suggesting the acute selling pressure is fading even if directional conviction remains absent.

## Implications by Asset Category

For equities, the defensive posture favors quality and low-volatility factors over high-beta growth. The index sitting below both major moving averages means trend-following strategies remain short or sidelined. A reclaim of the two hundred-day average near six thousand five hundred ninety-two would be the first technical signal of stabilization.

For fixed income, credit spreads holding steady rather than widening further is a constructive sign. Duration remains attractive if the risk-off mood deepens, but the flat spread trajectory suggests the credit market is not yet pricing recession risk.

For crypto, the elevated correlation to equities means Bitcoin is unlikely to rally independently unless a specific catalyst breaks the macro linkage. The zero point seven four correlation reading argues for treating crypto as a leveraged equity beta rather than a diversifier in the current regime.

For commodities and real assets, the weakening dollar provides a tailwind, and real estate investment trusts may benefit from a flight to yield if equity volatility persists. Energy names remain sensitive to geopolitical developments that have driven recent decoupling episodes.

The key watchpoint for a potential regime transition toward TRANSITION_UP is whether the VIX continues declining below twenty, high yield spreads begin narrowing, and the S and P 500 reclaims its two hundred-day moving average. Those three signals in combination would justify reclassifying to a more constructive posture.
