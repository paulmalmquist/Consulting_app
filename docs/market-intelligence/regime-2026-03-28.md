# Market Regime Report — March twenty-eighth, twenty twenty-six

## Classification: RISK OFF DEFENSIVE

Confidence: High. No regime change from prior session.

The market remains firmly in a risk-off defensive posture for the seventh consecutive session. The Iran conflict continues to dominate the macro narrative, keeping equities under pressure, volatility elevated, and the dollar bid. None of the six core signals show meaningful improvement from yesterday's readings, and the weight of evidence points to this regime persisting into next week absent a geopolitical de-escalation catalyst.

## Signal Summary

The S and P five hundred closed at roughly sixty-five oh six, sitting well below both its fifty-day moving average of sixty-five seventy and its two hundred-day moving average of sixty-seven fifty. That is a bearish configuration by any technical standard — price below both trend filters with the fifty-day itself rolling below the two hundred-day, confirming a structural downtrend rather than a momentary dip.

The VIX printed at twenty-seven point four four, elevated but not at panic levels. With the index sitting comfortably above twenty-five for more than a week, the market is pricing sustained uncertainty rather than a one-off shock. The term structure is likely flat to mildly inverted given this VIX level, consistent with persistent near-term hedging demand rather than calm complacency.

High yield credit spreads on the ICE BofA index came in at three point one seven percent, with double-B paper at one point nine six and single-B at three point four nine. These are moderately wide by twenty twenty-five standards but not distressed. The gradual widening trend confirms credit markets are repricing risk in sympathy with equities, though the move has been orderly so far.

Bitcoin and the S and P five hundred are once again moving in lockstep, with the thirty-day correlation coefficient climbing to zero point seven four. After a brief decoupling episode earlier in March, crypto has re-synchronized with equities under macro stress. This high correlation is a textbook risk-off signal — diversification benefits evaporate when everything sells together.

The DXY dollar index firmed to one hundred point two one, up nearly two percent over the past month. The dollar is catching a classic safe-haven bid from Iran escalation fears and rising oil prices. A strengthening dollar adds headwinds for commodities, emerging markets, and multinational earnings.

M two money supply continues expanding at four point nine percent year over year, reaching roughly twenty-two point seven trillion dollars. Demand deposits are up nearly nine percent and retail money market assets hit a record two point one six trillion. This monetary expansion provides a floor under financial conditions and should eventually support risk assets, but for now the geopolitical overhang is overwhelming the liquidity tailwind.

## Regime Stability

This is the seventh day in the RISK OFF DEFENSIVE regime, which was triggered on March twenty-second when the S and P broke below its two hundred-day moving average. The prior regime was RISK ON MOMENTUM. Confidence in the current classification remains high because five of six signals point clearly defensive, with only M two expansion as a contrarian positive.

## Asset Category Implications

For equities, the defensive posture favors quality, low-beta, and defense and aerospace names over cyclicals and growth. The defense sector has been paradoxically strong despite the broader risk-off tone, reflecting direct revenue exposure to the conflict.

For fixed income, Treasury yields remain anchored by flight-to-quality flows. Credit should be approached cautiously with spreads still widening. Duration is an acceptable hedge in this regime.

For crypto, the re-coupling with equities means Bitcoin and major altcoins offer limited diversification. Positioning should be reduced or hedged until the correlation breaks back down.

For currencies, the dollar strength regime favors dollar-denominated assets and penalizes carry trades and emerging market FX. DXY above one hundred is a key level to watch.

For commodities, oil remains bid on conflict premium while industrial metals face headwinds from the strong dollar and growth concerns. Gold should perform well as a dual beneficiary of geopolitical fear and real rate uncertainty.
