# Regime Classification — March 29, 2026

**Current regime: RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE**
**Prior regime: RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE (unchanged since March 22)**
**Confidence: High**

## Signal Summary

The S and P 500 closed Friday at roughly sixty-five ninety-two, sitting about eight and three-quarters percent below its all-time high from late January. The index has reclaimed its fifty-day moving average at sixty-five forty-seven but remains firmly below the two-hundred-day at sixty-seven forty-two, a level it lost on March nineteenth. That gap between the fifty-day and two-hundred-day lines — with the shorter average below the longer — is a textbook bearish alignment that has persisted for over a week now.

Volatility remains elevated. The VIX closed Thursday at thirty-one point zero five, up over thirteen percent on the session. More notably, the VIX is trading above the three-month VIX3M at twenty-nine point two seven, putting the term structure in backwardation. When near-term implied volatility exceeds longer-dated vol, it signals the market is pricing acute near-term stress rather than a gradual grind lower. This inversion has been a reliable hallmark of defensive regimes historically.

High yield credit spreads on the ICE BofA index sit at three hundred seventeen basis points. That is meaningfully wider than the sub-three-hundred levels from early in the year but well short of panic territory above five hundred. Spreads are widening directionally, consistent with rising risk aversion, but the move has been orderly rather than dislocating.

The Bitcoin and S and P 500 correlation has been volatile throughout March. The thirty-day reading spiked to zero point seven four earlier in the month as both sold off together, but more recently the twenty-day correlation dipped toward negative zero point five as Bitcoin decoupled on idiosyncratic flows. This mixed signal — periods of macro-driven co-movement interspersed with crypto-specific divergence — is typical of defensive regimes where correlations are unstable.

The dollar index closed near one hundred point two, up about one point nine percent over the past month. The strengthening reflects a modest flight-to-safety bid amid geopolitical tensions, particularly the Iran conflict and rising oil prices. Over twelve months the dollar is still weaker by about three point seven percent, so this is a tactical bid within a broader weakening trend.

M2 money supply grew four point nine percent year-over-year in February, up from four point three percent in January. This modest expansion provides some baseline support for asset prices but is nowhere near the pace that would fuel a risk-on breakout.

## Regime Confirmation

All six signals confirm RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE. The S and P 500 is below its two-hundred-day moving average with bearish alignment between the fifty and two-hundred-day lines. The VIX is elevated above thirty with an inverted term structure. Credit spreads are widening but orderly. The dollar is strengthening on safety flows. Correlations are unstable. And M2 growth is tepid. None of these signals approach panic thresholds — the VIX would need to spike above forty, spreads above five hundred, and correlations would need to converge toward one simultaneously for a RISK_OFF_PANIC classification.

## Implications by Asset Category

For equities, the defensive regime favors quality, low-beta, and dividend names over growth and momentum. The two-hundred-day moving average at sixty-seven forty-two is the key level to watch for any regime transition upward.

For crypto, the decoupling from equities creates a window where idiosyncratic catalysts matter more than macro. However, elevated VIX and risk-off positioning mean any sharp equity decline could quickly pull crypto lower through forced selling and margin calls.

For fixed income, the combination of a thirty-one VIX and three-seventeen spreads suggests duration is acting as a hedge but credit is under mild stress. Favor investment grade over high yield in this regime.

For commodities, the geopolitical premium in oil and the mild dollar strengthening create a tug-of-war. Defensive commodities like gold benefit from uncertainty, while industrial commodities face headwinds from the growth slowdown signal embedded in equity weakness.

For currencies, the dollar's tactical bid should persist as long as geopolitical risk remains elevated, but the structural weakening over twelve months suggests any de-escalation could reverse the recent gains quickly.
