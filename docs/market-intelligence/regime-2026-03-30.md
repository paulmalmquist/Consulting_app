# Regime Classification — March 30, 2026

**Regime: RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE (unchanged)**
**Confidence: High**
**Prior regime: RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE since March 22**

The market regime remains firmly risk-off defensive heading into the final trading day of March. This marks the ninth consecutive day in this classification, and the signals have actually intensified rather than faded since last week.

## Signal Summary

The S and P 500 closed Friday near five thousand five hundred ninety-two, sitting right at its two hundred day simple moving average of five thousand five hundred ninety-two and well below its fifty day exponential moving average of five thousand seven hundred ninety-one. The index is essentially testing its long-term trend support, which is a critical technical juncture. A sustained break below would shift the conversation toward RISK_OFF_PANIC territory.

The VIX spiked to thirty-one point zero five on March twenty-seventh, up over thirteen percent in a single session. More importantly, the VIX is trading above VIX3M at twenty-nine point twenty-seven, putting the term structure in backwardation. This inversion signals that near-term fear exceeds medium-term expectations, a classic stress indicator that typically accompanies sharp drawdowns or geopolitical shocks. The Iran conflict remains the dominant catalyst.

High yield credit spreads on the ICE BofA index sit at three hundred seventeen basis points for the broad index, with BB spreads at one hundred ninety-six basis points and single-B at three hundred forty-nine basis points. These levels are elevated relative to the tight prints from late twenty twenty-five but remain well below crisis thresholds. The CCC tier continues to show bifurcation, with yesterday's rotation brief noting nine hundred forty-five basis points at the lowest quality tier.

Bitcoin and the S and P 500 thirty-day correlation has surged to zero point seventy-four, the highest reading of twenty twenty-six. This confirms a macro-driven regime where crypto is trading as a risk asset rather than a diversifier. The brief decoupling episode from earlier in March has fully reversed. When BTC-SPX correlation runs this high, it means institutional flows are treating both asset classes as a single risk bucket.

The dollar index closed at one hundred point three two on March twenty-ninth, strengthening nearly two percent over the past month. Geopolitical safe-haven demand from the Iran situation and scaled-back Fed rate cut expectations are supporting the greenback. The Fed still signals one twenty-five basis point cut for twenty twenty-six, but traders are growing skeptical even of that.

M2 money supply grew four point nine percent year over year in February, accelerating slightly from four point three percent in January. The absolute level reached twenty-two point seven trillion dollars. This moderate expansion provides a liquidity floor but is not aggressive enough to fuel a risk-on reversal on its own.

## Regime Change Assessment

No regime change today. The RISK_OFF_DEFENSIVE classification holds with high confidence. The key thresholds to watch for a potential shift are as follows. A move toward RISK_OFF_PANIC would require VIX sustaining above forty, HY spreads blowing past four hundred fifty basis points, and SPX breaking decisively below the two hundred day moving average with high volume. A move toward TRANSITION_UP would require VIX declining below twenty-two, spreads narrowing below two hundred eighty basis points, and SPX reclaiming its fifty day moving average with breadth improvement.

## Implications by Asset Category

Equities remain in a defensive posture. Quality and low-volatility factors should continue to outperform. Cyclicals and high-beta names face headwinds from both the elevated VIX and the strengthening dollar. The two hundred day moving average test is the key level — a bounce would be constructive, a break would accelerate selling.

Fixed income benefits from the flight to quality. The yield curve dynamics noted in yesterday's rates brief — uninversion via rising short-end rates — add a recession signal on top of the geopolitical stress. Duration positioning favors the long end as a hedge.

Crypto remains tethered to the macro regime at this correlation level. The five-signal bottom convergence in BTC noted in recent briefs is interesting from a structural standpoint, but in a high-correlation macro regime, crypto cannot rally sustainably without equities participating. Position sizing should reflect this dependency.

Commodities are bifurcated. Energy benefits from Iran conflict premium and dollar strength is partially offset by demand concerns. Gold continues to serve as the primary chaos hedge with both geopolitical and monetary tailwinds.

Currencies favor defensive positioning. Dollar strength persists on safe-haven flows, compressing EM and commodity currencies. The yen may outperform as a secondary safe haven if risk-off deepens.
