## Key Findings

First, Aave at one trillion dollars in cumulative loans is a legitimacy milestone for decentralized finance that should not be dismissed. This is not a small-scale experiment — the lending infrastructure at this scale rivals some mid-tier traditional financial institutions in throughput.

Second, Sky Protocol's 24-percent weekly TVL surge signals that the protocol rebranding and repositioning strategy is working. Watch for continued TVL growth in Sky as it captures share from legacy Maker collateralization pools.

Third, the BGD Labs departure from Aave on April 1 introduces real governance execution risk. BGD Labs handled a significant portion of Aave's technical infrastructure and upgrade pipeline. The transition period through Q2 2026 should be monitored for protocol risk and potential smart contract exposure windows.

Fourth, yield-bearing stablecoins doubling in supply represents the highest-conviction structural trend in DeFi this cycle. Products that deliver stability, predictability, and yield in a single instrument are pulling capital from both traditional fixed income and speculation-oriented DeFi strategies.

Fifth, the gap between DeFi lending rates and the Fed funds rate at 3.50 to 3.75 percent is narrow — Aave at 4 to 6 percent offers modest spread over the risk-free rate. If the Fed cuts in H2 2026, that spread widens and DeFi lending becomes relatively more attractive.
