# Sales Positioning Angles — 2026-03-20

---

## Against Cherre

**Their claim:** "Real Estate's Leading Data Management Platform — collects, standardizes, validates, and supercharges data across 150+ integrations and 3.3B+ addresses."

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"Your team doesn't need another data pipeline — they need an AI that already knows the difference between a preferred return and a catch-up provision."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
Cherre is excellent at solving the data plumbing problem — getting data from Point A to Point B in a clean format. But clean data is table stakes, not competitive advantage. The firms that outperform don't win because their data is cleaner — they win because they act on it faster. Winston operates at the decision layer: when your CFO asks why NOI dropped, Winston doesn't point to a dashboard — it explains the drivers, flags the DSCR covenant risk, and recommends an action. That's the gap between a data platform and an investment environment.

**Objection this handles:**
"We've already invested heavily in our data infrastructure with Cherre. Why would we need Winston?"

## Against Cherre (Agent STUDIO specific)

**Their claim:** "Agent STUDIO empowers real estate organizations to design, deploy, and scale purpose-built AI agents using their connected data."

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"Cherre gives you a toolkit to build your own AI. Winston already built it — trained on REPE workflows, waterfall structures, and LP agreements."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
Agent STUDIO is a powerful concept, but it shifts the burden to your team. Someone at your firm needs to design the agent, define the workflow, test the output, and maintain it as your data changes. That's a technology project, not a productivity gain. Winston ships with domain-specific intelligence out of the box — it knows how to calculate DSCR, model waterfalls, and generate LP reports without anyone writing a prompt or composing an agent workflow. The question isn't whether AI agents are valuable — it's whether your team should be building them or using them.

**Objection this handles:**
"Cherre just launched Agent STUDIO — we can build our own AI agents on top of our data."

## Against Dealpath

**Their claim:** "Real estate's most trusted deal management platform — centralize data, surface insights, and execute efficiently across the investment lifecycle."

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"Dealpath manages your deals until closing day — Winston manages your investments from sourcing through the last LP distribution."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
Dealpath is excellent at the front end of the investment lifecycle — sourcing, screening, and deal execution. But the moment a deal closes, your team moves to a completely different set of tools: Excel for asset management, another system for LP reporting, email for investor communications, and manual processes for waterfall calculations. Every handoff is a data gap and a risk. Winston is one environment across the entire lifecycle — the same AI that screens your deal also manages the asset, calculates the waterfall, and generates your LP reports. No migration, no broken links, no reconciliation between systems.

**Objection this handles:**
"We already use Dealpath for our deal pipeline and we're happy with it."

## Against Dealpath (AI Studio specific)

**Their claim:** "AI Studio: Accelerate deal screening, comps, and underwriting with AI purpose-built for institutional CRE."

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"Dealpath's AI screens deals faster. Winston's AI screens deals AND tells you what they do to your fund returns, LP distributions, and portfolio concentration."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
Dealpath AI Studio is pragmatic — it speeds up OM abstraction, recommends comps, and screens deals. Those are valuable time-savers. But they're point solutions operating in a deal management silo. When Winston screens a deal, it knows your fund's existing portfolio — your current industrial allocation, your geographic concentration, your return targets, your LP commitments. It doesn't just tell you the deal looks good — it tells you whether the deal makes your fund better. That's the difference between faster deal screening and smarter investment decisions.

**Objection this handles:**
"Dealpath just launched AI Studio and we're rolling it out. Do we really need another AI?"

## Against Both (General Positioning)

**Their claim:** Both Cherre and Dealpath position as the "platform" or "source of truth" for real estate data and deals.

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"Platforms give you infrastructure. Winston gives you answers — in the language your LPs and lenders actually speak."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
The real estate technology market is full of platforms — data platforms, deal platforms, analytics platforms. Every one requires your team to translate platform output into the actual artifacts your business needs: IC memos, LP reports, covenant compliance packages, board presentations. Winston skips the translation step. Ask it a question, get an answer in the format your audience needs. That's not a platform — that's a teammate who knows your business.

**Objection this handles:**
"We're trying to consolidate platforms, not add another one."
