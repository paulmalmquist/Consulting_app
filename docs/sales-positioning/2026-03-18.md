# Sales Positioning Angles — 2026-03-18

*Derived from: Altus Group / ARGUS Intelligence, Cambio, Glean*

---

## Against Altus Group / ARGUS Intelligence

**Their claim:** "One platform transforming how you model, analyze and optimize your portfolio"

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"ARGUS tells you what your model says. Winston tells you what your portfolio means — and then acts on it."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
ARGUS Intelligence is a modeling and analytics environment. It's excellent at what it does — it's the industry standard for DCF modeling and it's been that way for 30 years. But when your CFO needs to know why Q3 NOI came in $200K below underwriting, or your GP needs to draft the LP communication by end of day, ARGUS hands the work back to your analyst. Winston handles the question in the conversation — it reads your models, runs the analysis, and drafts the output.

**Objection this handles:**
"We already use ARGUS / ARGUS is the industry standard."
→ Response: "Absolutely — ARGUS is where your models live. Winston is where you run the firm. They're complementary. Winston can read ARGUS exports and use them as inputs. But ARGUS can't close the loop from model to LP communication to action."

## Against Cambio

**Their claim:** "AI powered commercial real estate operations for asset managers" — "agentic AI that reasons across unstructured data, runs multi-step analyses, and continuously adapts"

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"Cambio is an AI sustainability officer. Winston is your AI operating partner — from deal entry to LP distribution."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
Cambio does one thing well: it automates ESG compliance and building data collection for large institutional owners. It's genuinely useful if you have a dedicated sustainability reporting requirement and a portfolio of 500+ buildings. But if you're a REPE GP managing five to fifteen assets and your most acute pain is fund performance visibility, LP reporting, and deal underwriting — Cambio addresses none of that. Winston was built for the GP's office, not the sustainability team.

**Objection this handles:**
"We're looking at AI tools for asset management" — when the prospect has a broad brief and might lump all "AI for real estate" together.
→ Response: "What's the specific workflow that's costing you the most time right now — LP communications, underwriting reviews, tracking covenant compliance? Let's start there. The right AI for your firm depends entirely on what your bottleneck is."

## Against Glean

**Their claim:** "The horizontal AI platform for enterprise superintelligence" — "Work AI that Works"

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"Glean searches your company. Winston knows your firm — the fund structure, the LP agreements, the waterfall math. Retrieval is not understanding."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
Glean is impressive technology — it indexes everything across your 100 SaaS apps and makes it searchable with AI. But a REPE firm doesn't have a search problem. You have a synthesis and execution problem: your data lives in Excel models, your LP agreements have specific terms that govern every distribution, and your analyst needs to know not just where the document is, but what the preferred return hurdle means for this quarter's cash available for distribution. Winston was built with that domain knowledge baked in — Glean cannot learn it from indexing your files.

**Objection this handles:**
"We're evaluating enterprise AI platforms broadly" — when a prospect is being pitched horizontal AI by IT or a large vendor.
→ Response: "Horizontal AI gives you better search. Domain-specific AI gives you a second analyst who already understands private equity. For your firm's most important workflows — fund management, LP reporting, deal underwriting — ask the horizontal AI platform what a preferred return waterfall looks like. Then ask Winston."

## Against the 'We'll Just Use ChatGPT / General AI' Objection

*(Not a specific competitor but informed by the Glean horizontal AI positioning)*

**Their implicit claim:** "General purpose AI models are good enough for our needs"

**Novendor angle (one sentence):**
"General AI knows what a waterfall is. Winston knows *your* waterfall — the specific terms in your LP agreements, the actual fund structure, the current account balances."

**Elaboration (2-3 sentences for a sales call):**
General-purpose AI is excellent for drafting emails and summarizing documents. The moment you need it to calculate a GP promote based on the specific hurdle rate and catch-up provisions in your Fund II LPA, it starts hallucinating. Winston is connected to your actual fund data, understands your specific structure, and has 83 tools that take action on that data — it doesn't just answer questions, it does the work.

**Objection this handles:**
"We already use Claude / ChatGPT for research and writing — isn't that enough?"
→ Response: "For general research and writing, yes — those tools are excellent. For fund management, the question isn't whether the AI can write well. It's whether the AI knows your fund. Winston does."
