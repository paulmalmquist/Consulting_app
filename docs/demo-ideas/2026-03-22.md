### Demo 1: AI Deal Scoring Pipeline — From Broker Email to Ranked Shortlist in 2 Minutes

**Tagline:** A broker sends you 5 deals. Winston scores, ranks, and tells you which one to call back about — before your acquisitions team opens the attachments.

**Target persona:** Head of Acquisitions / VP of Investments

**Problem it solves:** Dealpath Connect now has all three major global brokerages on its marketplace — meaning institutional deal flow is consolidating onto one platform. But getting deals isn't the bottleneck; triaging them is. A mid-market REPE shop receiving 200–400 OMs per year still relies on analysts spending 30–45 minutes per deal on initial screening — pulling cap rates, checking market comps, assessing fit against fund strategy. At 20 deals per week, that's 10–15 analyst-hours on first-pass triage alone. Winston's Deal Radar + AI scoring pipeline collapses this to minutes.

**Demo flow (7 steps):**
1. Open the Deal Radar — show the current pipeline view with active deals scored and ranked
2. Point out the scoring dimensions: cap rate vs. fund target, market fundamentals, asset type fit, risk flags, return potential
3. Click into a top-scored deal — show the deal detail page with the full breakdown: property summary, financials, scoring rationale
4. Open Winston AI chat: *"We just got 3 new deals from CBRE — a 200-unit multifamily in Austin, a 50K SF industrial in Phoenix, and a mixed-use in Nashville. Our fund targets 14%+ IRR, multifamily and industrial only, Sun Belt markets. Rank these by fit."*
5. Winston responds with a ranked assessment: deal-by-deal fit score, flags the mixed-use as outside strategy, highlights the industrial as strongest fit given current portfolio concentration
6. Ask: *"Run a quick scenario on the Phoenix industrial — what IRR range if we assume a 5.5% entry cap and 100 bps exit expansion?"*
7. Winston triggers the scenario engine and returns an IRR range with probability distribution — all within the chat

**Winston capabilities shown:**
- Deal Radar with pipeline tracking and scoring (deployed)
- Deal detail with multi-dimensional scoring (deployed)
- AI chat → deal triage and ranking via natural language
- Scenario engine triggered from chat (deployed)
- Portfolio-aware recommendations (checks current holdings for concentration)

**The "wow moment":** Three deals described in plain English get scored, ranked, and the winner gets a quick IRR scenario — all in under 90 seconds in the chat. The acquisitions VP sees Winston functioning as a junior analyst who already knows the fund's strategy.

**Data needed:** Deal Radar with seeded pipeline data (exists). Scenario engine (deployed). Fund strategy parameters for portfolio-aware scoring (configurable).

**Build status:** READY — Deal Radar, deal scoring, deal detail, scenario engine v1+v2, and the AI chat workspace are all deployed. No code changes needed.

**Sales angle:** Direct counter to Dealpath Connect's network moat. Message: "Dealpath connects you to deals. Winston tells you which ones to pursue." This positions Winston as the intelligence layer that works regardless of where deal flow originates — Dealpath, brokers, or direct sourcing.

### Demo 2: Capital Projects Draw Audit — Construction Oversight That Catches Overcharges

**Tagline:** Winston audits your contractor's draw request against the budget, flags variances, and tells you what to challenge — before you sign the check.

**Target persona:** CFO / VP of Development at REPE firms with active construction or value-add programs

**Problem it solves:** REPE firms running value-add or development strategies manage capital projects with draw requests from contractors — typically monthly pay applications with 50–200 line items. A single miscoded or inflated line item can cost $50K–$500K on a $20M renovation. Today, asset managers spot-check draws manually in Excel, catching maybe 60% of issues. The rest get approved and buried. Winston's capital projects engine processes the full draw, compares every line to the approved budget, and flags variances before approval.

**Demo flow (7 steps):**
1. Open the Capital Projects module — show active projects with budget, spent-to-date, and completion percentage
2. Click into a project — show the budget breakdown by category (hard costs, soft costs, contingency)
3. Navigate to the Draw Management tab — show the current draw request with line-item detail
4. Winston's draw auditing automatically flags: 2 line items over budget by >10%, 1 category where cumulative spend is ahead of schedule (potential front-loading), and a soft cost that wasn't in the original budget
5. Click into a flagged line — show the variance detail: budgeted amount, requested amount, cumulative overage, and Winston's risk assessment
6. Open Winston AI chat: *"Summarize the issues with Draw #4 and draft a response to the contractor requesting backup documentation for the flagged items."*
7. Winston generates a structured response letter: lists each flagged item, the variance, and the specific documentation requested — ready to send

**Winston capabilities shown:**
- Capital Projects management (26KB service, deployed)
- Draw processing and pay app variance analysis (deployed)
- Draw auditing with automatic flag generation (deployed)
- AI-drafted contractor correspondence from audit findings
- Budget-to-actual tracking at line-item level

**The "wow moment":** The CFO sees Winston catch a $87K soft cost that wasn't in the original budget — a charge that would have been approved in a manual review because it was buried on page 4 of a 12-page draw request.

**Data needed:** Capital project with budget and draw history (can be seeded). Draw request with realistic line items and planted variances for demo impact. Draw auditing service is deployed.

**Build status:** READY — Capital Projects service (26KB), draw management, pay app variance analysis, and draw auditing are all deployed per Capability Inventory. AI chat can generate correspondence today. No code changes needed.

**Sales angle:** No REPE competitor offers AI-powered draw auditing. Yardi's construction module is accounting-focused, not intelligence-focused. Procore is for GCs, not owners.
