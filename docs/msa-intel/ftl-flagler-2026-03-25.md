# Zone Intelligence Brief: Fort Lauderdale — Flagler Village/FAT Village
**Run Date:** 2026-03-25 | **Asset Class:** Multifamily | **Tier:** 1

---

## Composite Acquisition Score: 3.65 / 10

> **Verdict:** Watch-and-wait. Flagler Village has outstanding long-term demand fundamentals — strong in-migration, rapid downtown population growth, Florida's favorable regulatory environment, and active capital markets. However, a massive near-term supply wave (1,525+ units delivering in 2026–2027) is already suppressing rents, and distress-driven acquisition opportunities are essentially absent. This is a market to underwrite for 2027–2028 entry, not today.

## 3d. Rent & Occupancy — Score: 5/10 (Decelerating)

Supply pressure is showing up in rent data. The market is in active supply digestion.

- **Average Flagler Village rent:** $2,824/month (Dec 2025) — **down 4.1% YoY** from $2,943
- **Studio:** $2,206/month (+11% YoY — countertrend, driven by strong young professional demand)
- **1 BR:** $3,121/month (−7% YoY)
- **2 BR:** $4,535/month (−2% YoY)
- **Concession level:** Light-to-moderate; move-in specials visible on listing platforms
- **Renter occupancy of households:** 63% of Flagler Village households are renter-occupied

The divergence between studio performance (+11%) and 1BR/2BR softness suggests new supply skews toward larger units, creating a relative compression on mid-size units.

**Gap flagged:** No occupancy rate by property class (Class A/B/C) available from free sources.

## 3f. Regulatory & Political — Score: 9/10 (Highly Favorable)

Florida remains the most landlord-friendly large state in the U.S.

- **Rent control:** Zero risk. Florida state law preempts local rent control ordinances (since 1977; periodically reaffirmed). No proposal pathway exists.
- **Opportunity Zones:** Flagler Village is in an active OZ. A 15-parcel assemblage on N. Andrews Avenue was listed specifically as an OZ deal. CEDARst Companies has an OZ project in the submarket with 215 apartments. QOF investment continues through at least 2026.
- **Impact fees:** No material changes found for 2025–2026.
- **Property taxes:** No significant reassessment cycle found; Broward County tax environment stable.
- **City government:** Fort Lauderdale is actively pro-development and has streamlined major projects like FAT Village through approvals.

## Key Findings

1. **⚠️ HIGH** — Supply tsunami: 1,525+ units delivering 2026–2027. FAT Village alone (600 units, $500M) represents the largest urban residential delivery in Flagler Village's history. This is the single most important factor suppressing the acquisition score.

2. **⚠️ HIGH** — Rents already declining: −4.1% YoY in Flagler Village with light-to-moderate concessions. 1BR units down 7% YoY. Market is in active supply digestion.

3. **✅ HIGH** — Record capital activity: $223.5M bridge loan (March 2026) is a strong institutional confidence signal despite near-term supply headwinds.

4. **✅ HIGH** — Structural demand is exceptional: 26–28% downtown population growth projected by 2030, sustained high-income in-migration from top U.S. metros, professional employment growing 6%+ YoY.

5. **✅ MEDIUM** — Best regulatory environment in the U.S.: No rent control risk, active OZ incentives, pro-development city government.

6. **✅ MEDIUM** — Positive leverage spread: 6.27% cap rate vs. 5.11% commercial mortgage rate — narrow but positive.

7. **⚡ MEDIUM** — Studio rents bucking the trend (+11% YoY): Young professional demand for smaller units is absorbing efficiently. Investors targeting studios or micro-units may find relative opportunity during the supply absorption cycle.

## Acquisition Recommendation

**Do not acquire stabilized multifamily assets in Flagler Village at current pricing today.** The combination of active supply delivery (1,525+ units through 2027), declining rents (−4% YoY), and minimal distress leaves no acquisition edge at market prices.

**Watch for 2027–2028 entry:** Once the supply wave is absorbed and concessions burn off, this market's structural demand case (in-migration, employment growth, OZ activity, downtown density growth) positions it for a strong rent recovery cycle. A buy-on-the-dip strategy targeting 2027 acquisitions at post-supply-compression pricing could offer meaningful upside.

**Opportunistic play today:** Studio-focused or micro-unit properties may outperform during the absorption cycle (+11% YoY studio rents). If a small studio-heavy building is available at compressed pricing, the unit mix offsets some supply pressure.
