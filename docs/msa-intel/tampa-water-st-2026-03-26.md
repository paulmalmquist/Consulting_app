## Transaction Activity (7/10 — Stable)

Tampa Bay's top CRE deals in 2025 generated $1.46B across all asset classes. The multifamily market is Florida's most transacted with $2.3B in trailing 12-month sales volume. Large-scale transactions rebounded with 20+ deals over $50M in 2024 (up from 13 in 2023), including four $100M+ trades. Retail saw 631 transactions at an average 6.3% cap rate and $301/SF. Water Street-specific transaction comps are limited in public data, but institutional buyer activity across the broader downtown market is robust.

## Supply Pipeline (6/10 — Moderating)

Water Street is in late-stage buildout of its $3B, 56-acre master plan targeting 2027 completion (3,500 residences, 2M SF office, 1M SF retail, two hotels). Near-term deliveries include The Stevedore at Gasworx (early 2026), Gasworx signature office tower (late 2026), and Luisa/Olivette residential (2027). A new entertainment district with a 3,500-seat live music venue, hotel, retail, and dining has been announced. Channel District infrastructure improvements across 9 street segments are on track for early 2027 completion. Metro-wide multifamily deliveries are dropping sharply from ~7,400 units (2025) to ~3,500 units (2026), providing meaningful supply relief. The City's Land Development Code update is expected to finalize in H2 2026.

## Demand Drivers (8/10 — Accelerating)

Tampa's private sector added 15,500 jobs year-over-year in June 2025, ranking third among FL metro areas. Lightcast ranks Tampa #8 nationally among large metros for talent attraction, specifically for prime-age, high-earning, college-educated workers. The Tampa Bay region is projected to add 397K-547K new residents between 2025 and 2030, and ranks #1 in net migration for young professionals and entrepreneurs. The Tampa Bay EDC has helped attract companies creating approximately 50,000 direct jobs over 16 years across financial services, life sciences, healthcare, and advanced manufacturing.

## Rent & Occupancy (6/10 — Stabilizing)

Class A multifamily is averaging $2,305/unit (+1.4% YoY), Class B at $1,801 (+0.8%), and Class C at $1,479 (+1.7%). Metro occupancy sits at 94.6% (August 2025), slightly below the national average of 94.7%, but vacancy is expected to decline 60bps to close 2025 at 6.6%, ending three consecutive years of increases. Premium downtown office (4 & 5 Star) achieves $47+/SF, with Water Street and Midtown West asking $60-70/SF. The Downtown Tampa South - Islands submarket has the lowest office vacancy at 2.92%. Supply tapering in 2026 is expected to support stronger rent growth.

## Capital Markets (6/10 — Cautiously Improving)

The CBRE Lending Momentum Index rose 112% YoY in Q3 2025, the highest since 2018. National CMBS issuance reached $115.2B through November 2025, the highest since 2007. However, CMBS delinquency sits at 7.14% (Feb 2026) with overall distress at 11.98%, and office delinquency exceeds 12%. A $100B+ CMBS maturity wall in 2026 creates refinancing risk, though Tampa multifamily is viewed as a supply-constrained safe harbor with $1.7B in investment volume (2025, second-highest in FL). Prime rate stands at 6.75% as of December 2025, with lenders tightening underwriting but activity increasing overall.

## Regulatory & Political (7/10 — Mixed)

Florida's state preemption of local rent control maintains a landlord-friendly environment. Tampa has 23 designated Opportunity Zones, but federal OZ benefits sunset after 2026, creating urgency for OZ-eligible plays. Tampa's multimodal impact fees are updating for the first time since 1989, jumping from $955 to $5,284 for a 2,000 SF home, phasing in at 25% starting June 2026. This will meaningfully increase development costs and slow future supply, benefiting existing asset holders. Hillsborough County extended its 0.5% infrastructure surtax through December 2041. The FL legislature is considering significant property tax relief proposals. Tampa's Future Land Use Update has been continued to incorporate SB 180 (anti-restrictive zoning legislation).

## Key Findings

1. **Acquisition window opening** — Water Street/Channel District is in late-stage $3B master plan buildout nearing 2027 completion. Stabilized assets from initial phases are entering the market as the neighborhood proves out.

2. **Supply cliff ahead** — Multifamily deliveries dropping 53% from ~7,400 units (2025) to ~3,500 units (2026) metro-wide. Reduced supply + strong migration = rent growth acceleration.

3. **Structural demand tailwind** — Tampa ranks #1 in net migration for young professionals and #8 nationally for talent attraction. This is not cyclical — it's a sustained demographic shift.

4. **Premium validates positioning** — Water Street office commanding $60-70/SF vs. broader Tampa at $47/SF demonstrates the rent premium the neighborhood commands.

5. **Impact fee increase benefits incumbents** — 5x increase from $955 to $5,284 (phasing June 2026) raises the cost of future development and effectively moats existing assets.

6. **OZ urgency** — Federal OZ benefits sunset after 2026. Any remaining OZ-eligible acquisitions in Tampa's 23 zones require immediate evaluation.

7. **Distressed opportunities possible** — CMBS maturity wall and 12%+ office delinquency may surface distressed deals, though Water Street's premium positioning likely insulates its office assets.

8. **Entertainment district catalyst** — New 3,500-seat venue and mixed-use entertainment complex will significantly enhance the neighborhood's amenity profile and foot traffic.
