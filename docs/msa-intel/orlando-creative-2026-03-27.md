## Executive Summary

Orlando Creative Village/Parramore presents a mixed acquisition picture. The zone benefits from exceptional macroeconomic tailwinds — Orlando is the #1 fastest-growing major metro in the US for jobs, population, and GDP — and a highly favorable regulatory environment with active Opportunity Zone status, CRA support, and no rent control risk. However, the near-term picture is tempered by a heavy supply pipeline ($600M Phase 2 with 1,278 residential units) and localized rent softness (-0.1% YoY in Creative Village with concessions). Capital markets are expanding and transaction velocity is strong metro-wide, but the submarket-specific absorption risk from concentrated new deliveries is the primary drag on the composite score.

**Acquisition Thesis:** Attractive for patient capital with a 3-5 year hold. The supply wave will peak and taper, demand fundamentals are the strongest in the nation, and OZ + LIHTC stacking creates powerful tax-advantaged structures. Value-add plays on existing Class B stock may outperform new-build acquisitions in the near term given concession pressure on new product.

## Transaction Activity (Score: 7/10)

Orlando multifamily transaction activity increased 56% year-over-year in 2025, with Q3 quarterly volume reaching the highest level since 2022. Institutional buyers are returning as the median price per unit has declined approximately 25% from the 2022 peak, creating attractive entry points.

Within Creative Village specifically, the UnionWest sale in December 2024 was Orlando's most expensive multifamily transaction of the year, validating institutional demand for this submarket. JLL is actively marketing select parcels at Creative Village for future development. A recent comparable: a 216-unit suburban Orlando complex sold for $34.3M ($158,796/unit) in March 2026.

**Key signal:** Accelerating velocity with institutional re-entry, but specific Creative Village comp data is limited.

---

## Supply Pipeline (Score: 7/10 — HIGH RISK)

Creative Village Phase 2 is a $600M investment planned for 2026-2029 delivery, including:
- 450 market-rate apartments
- 106 mixed-income units
- 600 student housing beds
- The Beacon at Creative Village: 122 additional market-rate units (7-story mixed-use, proposed Dec 2024)

The broader Orlando metro absorbed 30,000 new multifamily units over the past two years. However, construction starts are tapering nationally and locally, which should moderate future supply risk beyond the current pipeline.

The Unlocked Open-Door Program targets 300+ vacant Parramore lots for single-family homes (not directly competing with multifamily supply), backed by $750K in CRA funding with permit fee rebates.

**Key signal:** Heavy near-term supply concentration in this specific subzone. Absorption is the critical variable.

## Demand Drivers (Score: 8/10)

Orlando claimed the "Triple Crown" — #1 among the 30 most populous US metros for job growth, population growth, and nominal GDP growth:
- 76,000 new residents added in 2024 (1,500 per week)
- 2.7% population growth rate (vs 2.0% statewide)
- 65% of recent growth from international migration
- Employment growth forecast at 1.3% in 2026 (vs 0.5% national)
- 1 million job openings projected 2026-2031

Major employer activity includes Orlando Health (570 jobs), KPMG US (195), EY (184), plus expansions from ThreatLocker, BNY Mellon, and Charles Schwab. The tech and financial services sectors are diversifying the employment base beyond tourism.

Creative Village is anchored by UCF Downtown and Valencia College, creating a built-in demand pool. SunRail and LYMMO transit connectivity are competitive advantages, with the proposed Sunshine Corridor (Airport to Disney Springs) potentially boosting ridership from 1M to 6M+ annually.

**Key signal:** Demand fundamentals are among the strongest in the nation. Population inflow alone creates substantial organic absorption.

## Rent & Occupancy (Score: 5/10)

Creative Village specifically is experiencing slight rent softness:
- Average rents: Studio $1,561, 1BR $1,729, 2BR $2,222
- Trailing 12-month rent change: -0.1% (submarket)
- Modera Creative Village offering up to 1 month free rent
- Metro-wide: Average asking rent $1,763

Metro-wide, rents are forecast to grow 2.0-2.4% by year-end. Stabilized occupancy edged up to 94.5% as of September (up 30 bps YoY) despite elevated deliveries. The gap between Creative Village's local softness and the broader metro's growth trajectory reflects the concentrated supply impact.

Rent growth is expected to accelerate in 2026-2027 as the supply pipeline tapers and renter demand continues absorbing new inventory.

**Key signal:** Near-term concession pressure in Creative Village; metro-wide fundamentals support recovery.

## Capital Markets (Score: 7/10)

Capital availability is expanding across the board:
- Multifamily lending projected to increase 21% to $399.2B in 2026
- Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac allocated ~20% increase in capped business
- CMBS issuance hit $115.2B through November 2025 (highest since 2007)
- AAA spreads tighter than the 2022-2023 stress period
- Florida multifamily cap rates at 5.5% (vs 5.2% national average)
- Cap rate compression expected toward the 5.0-5.5% range low end

The combination of expanding agency volume, CMBS liquidity, and returning institutional buyers creates a favorable financing environment for acquisitions.

**Key signal:** Strong lending tailwinds support transaction activity and pricing.

## Regulatory & Political (Score: 8/10)

The regulatory environment is exceptionally favorable:
- **No rent control risk:** Florida state law preempts local rent control measures
- **Active Opportunity Zone:** South Parramore is a designated federal OZ with proven OZ + LIHTC stacking (Parramore Oaks: $20M in tax credits and incentives)
- **CRA support:** Community Redevelopment Agency actively contributing debt and incentives to development
- **Unlocked Open-Door Program:** 100% rebate on impact and building permit fees for homes priced at $375K or less
- **Down payment assistance:** $45K available ($55K for first responders)
- **EPA cleanup:** Federal grants funded environmental remediation on 26 of 68 Creative Village acres
- **Property tax:** Orange County rate ~0.94% (near FL average), with 2026 state-level tax relief proposals pending

**Key signal:** Stacked incentives (OZ + LIHTC + CRA + permit rebates) create powerful tax-advantaged acquisition structures.
