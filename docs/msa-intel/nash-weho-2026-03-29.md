## Capital Markets (6/10)

The national CMBS market showed strength with $115.2B in issuance in 2025, the highest since 2007. However, delinquency rates remain elevated at 7.14% (Feb 2026), and over $100B in CMBS loans mature in 2026, creating both refinancing risk and distressed acquisition opportunities.

For a sponsor targeting WeHo, this means lenders will require stronger underwriting and sponsor track records, but financing is available for well-structured deals.

## Regulatory & Political (8/10)

Tennessee's statewide prohibition on rent control provides long-term certainty that Nashville cannot implement rent stabilization measures. The Metro Planning Commission passed new zoning codes in September 2025 that promote housing density, with WeHo properties specifically being up-zoned to allow 3-4 units where previously only 2 were permitted. The Wedgewood-Houston/Chestnut Hill Planning Study by Metro Nashville further guides development in a growth-oriented direction.

The 2022 freeze on non-owner-occupied STR permits in residential zones has the secondary effect of redirecting investor capital toward long-term rental strategies, which benefits conventional multifamily operators.
