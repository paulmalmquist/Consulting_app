## Executive Summary

Jacksonville's Brooklyn/LaVilla submarket is a **demand-story zone** with a moderate near-term acquisition profile. Exceptional population growth (fastest major FL metro), transformational institutional catalysts (UF graduate campus, JTA autonomous transit), and $8.8B in downtown development create strong long-term fundamentals. However, the near-term score is tempered by a significant supply pipeline (1,763+ units in the immediate zone), subdued transaction velocity ($584M metro multifamily sales through Oct 2025 vs $3.3B peak), and rent growth still recovering from 10 quarters of flat/negative performance. This is a zone to watch for value-add entry as the supply wave peaks and demand drivers mature through 2026-2027.

## Transaction Activity (Score: 4/10)

Metro Jacksonville multifamily sales volume has declined sharply from $3.3B in 2021 to $584M through October 2025, reflecting broader capital markets hesitancy. Q3 2025 showed $315M in quarterly volume, a potential inflection point. Limited transaction data exists specifically for Brooklyn/LaVilla, though Gateway Jax acquired 2.85 acres at 801 W Bay St in LaVilla for $4M (Oct 2024) and One Riverside in Brooklyn has moved to leasing after completing Phase 1. Stronger transaction activity is anticipated metro-wide in 2026 as rates stabilize.

## Supply Pipeline (Score: 6/10 — negative weight)

The immediate zone has a substantial supply pipeline of approximately 1,763+ units across multiple projects. Gateway Jax Pearl Square is the largest concentration, with 205 units topping out at 515 Pearl St (mid-2026 delivery) as part of a $500M project ultimately bringing 1,000+ apartments. One Riverside Phase 1 (271 units) is now leasing in Brooklyn, with Phase 2 (115 units) planned. A 286-unit mixed-use building broke ground at 425 Beaver St. Johnson Commons is adding 91 townhomes in LaVilla targeting summer 2026.

The 61% decline in metro multifamily construction permits provides some supply moderation going forward, but near-term deliveries remain elevated for this submarket.

## Demand Drivers (Score: 8/10)

This is the zone's standout category. The University of Florida securing 20+ acres in LaVilla for a graduate campus — with $50M+ in city funding, classes already started Fall 2025, and new construction beginning 2026 — is a transformational catalyst that will drive sustained demand for housing, retail, and services. Programs include law, medicine, engineering, and MBA.

JTA's NAVI autonomous transit system is already operational in LaVilla, with the $246M Phase 2 Skyway conversion to elevated Holon people movers planned to connect LaVilla to San Marco, Brooklyn, and Riverside. Phase 3 ($100M) extends into Brooklyn and Riverside on streets.

Jacksonville added 16,365 new residents between July 2023-2024 (10th nationally), absorbed 31,700 net in-migrants in 2024, and is projected as the fastest-growing major Florida metro through 2028. Education and health services led job growth with 8,600 new positions. Over 150 corporate HQs operate in the region.

## Rent & Occupancy (Score: 5/10)

Brooklyn commands premium rents at approximately $1,965/month for a 1BR, well above the metro average of $1,412-$1,495. Metro occupancy stands at 92.8% (slightly below national average). After 10 consecutive quarters of flat-to-negative rent performance through Q3 2025, the market is shifting toward recovery with 1-2% growth expected. Net absorption of 1,630 units through Q3 2025 demonstrates demand. Concessions remain light. The trajectory is positive but gains are modest.

## Capital Markets (Score: 7/10)

Capital conditions are improving. FHFA raised GSE multifamily lending caps 20.5% to $176B combined for 2026. Fannie and Freddie financed $151.6B in multifamily in 2025 (+25% YoY). CMBS delinquency is declining (7.14% in Feb 2026, down from 7.47% in January), and multifamily-specific delinquency edged down to 6.85%. Multifamily led the CRE transaction rebound nationally with 51.1% YoY volume increase in Q3 2025. Jacksonville is seen as offering cap-rate compression potential relative to South Florida. $936B in CRE mortgages maturing in 2026 creates significant refinancing activity.

## Regulatory & Political (Score: 7/10)

Jacksonville benefits from Florida's state preemption on rent control — no risk of local rent stabilization measures. The city has 21 designated Opportunity Zones (34 in Duval County), with LaVilla and Brooklyn within active OZ tracts. The City Council reduced the millage rate by 0.125 mills (1.1%) to 11.1919 for FY26, a modest tax relief signal. The pro-development posture is evident in the $50M+ public investment in the UF LaVilla campus, DIA's active support of the $6.5B downtown pipeline, and JTA's $400M U2C program. No impact fee increases identified.

## Acquisition Thesis

**Hold / Monitor** at current score of 5.2. The zone's demand story is compelling (score 8/10) but the near-term supply overhang (6/10 risk, negative weight) and still-recovering rent trajectory (5/10) limit immediate acquisition attractiveness. The optimal entry window likely emerges in late 2026 to mid-2027 as: (a) current supply pipeline delivers and gets absorbed, (b) UF campus construction creates visible neighborhood transformation, (c) NAVI/U2C Phase 2 advances, and (d) rent growth reaccelerates. Value-add opportunities in older Brooklyn stock could emerge sooner as new Class A supply pulls renters, temporarily softening Class B/C occupancy. Watch for distressed sales if any of the pipeline projects face construction loan maturity pressure.
