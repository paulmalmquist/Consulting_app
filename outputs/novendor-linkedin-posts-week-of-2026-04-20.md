## Monday, April 20 — ARGUS sunset

**Slug:** `argus-sunset-clock-running`
**Link target:** https://novendor.ai/novendor/articles/argus-sunset-clock-running

ARGUS Standalone is being sunset. Every REPE CFO I talk to is already getting the upgrade pitch from their rep.

Here's the mistake I see people about to make: treating this as a "pick the ARGUS replacement" decision.

The property DCF stopped being the hard part of the job a while ago. The hard part is the reporting layer above it. That's where analyst hours go, that's where LP trust lives, and that's the lever that actually moves your close cycle.

The ARGUS sunset is a good forcing function. Don't waste it on a like-for-like swap.

## Tuesday, April 21 — The 600-hour quarter

**Slug:** `600-hour-quarter`
**Link target:** https://novendor.ai/novendor/articles/600-hour-quarter

Six hundred analyst hours per quarter.

That's what reconciliation costs at a real $4B REPE fund I looked at last month. Fifteen weeks of a single analyst's time, every three months, just to prove the numbers in the reporting pack match the numbers in the GL.

It doesn't show up in any budget. It gets coded to "fund operations." You only see it when an analyst leaves.

The tying part of reconciliation is mechanical. It should run in seconds. What's left for the analyst is the exceptions, and there are rarely that many.

## Wednesday, April 22 — LPs care about the package

**Slug:** `lps-care-about-the-package`
**Link target:** https://novendor.ai/novendor/articles/lps-care-about-the-package

LPs don't care what accounting system you run. They care about the package.

The package has gotten harder. ILPA templates expanded. ESG expected. State pension mandates moving. What used to be a PDF is now a multi-file pack in specific formats, due two weeks after close, for fifteen or twenty LPs each with a slightly different version.

Most firms handle this with an IR director assembling it by hand. That works through Fund II. Around Fund III, the math breaks, and it breaks in a specific way: error compounding through reconciliation queries you can't answer fast enough.

## Thursday, April 23 — Multi-sector breaks Excel

**Slug:** `multi-sector-breaks-excel`
**Link target:** https://novendor.ai/novendor/articles/multi-sector-breaks-excel

Single-sector funds can live in Excel for a long time. Add a second sector and the workbook starts losing integrity. Add a third and it's not recoverable.

Multifamily occupancy is units over units. Storage occupancy is square feet over square feet and it's seasonal. Student housing resets in August. Senior care has regulatory KPIs your multifamily analyst has never seen.

Each sector is solvable on its own. The blending is where Excel finally breaks, and it usually breaks at one of three specific moments: analyst transition, LP audit, or Fund IV close.

## Friday, April 24 — AI-native vs AI-on-top

**Slug:** `ai-native-vs-ai-on-top`
**Link target:** https://novendor.ai/novendor/articles/ai-native-vs-ai-on-top

Every REPE vendor has shipped an AI feature in the last eighteen months. Juniper Square AI CRM. Dealpath Copilot. Yardi AI. They all look roughly the same on demo day.

There are two very different things being built under the same label.

One puts a language model in front of a data model that doesn't agree with itself. The AI summarizes cleanly but hallucinates on anything that crosses systems.

The other rebuilds the reporting layer so the authoritative state is queryable, lineage-traced, and locked per period. Then the AI is answering from something real.

The first reduces nothing. The second reduces your close cycle.
