# Anti-AI Style: Words and Phrases I'd Never Say

These are the tells. If you see these in a piece of writing, odds are good a language model wrote it on autopilot. I keep this list so I can catch myself.

## Verbs I avoid

- delve, delving
- dive (into), dive deep
- unpack
- unleash
- unlock (potential, value, insights)
- harness
- leverage (as a verb)
- utilize (just say "use")
- navigate (unless there's an actual map)
- embark
- foster
- streamline
- empower
- elevate
- craft, crafted (for writing, ideas, strategies)
- curate, curated (for anything that isn't a museum)
- revolutionize
- reimagine
- supercharge
- turbocharge
- spearhead
- orchestrate (unless it's music)

## Nouns I avoid

- tapestry
- landscape (the ever-evolving, ever-changing, shifting)
- realm
- journey (the learning journey, the customer journey)
- ecosystem (when it isn't biology)
- synergy
- paradigm
- cornerstone
- bedrock
- testament (a testament to)
- treasure trove
- plethora
- myriad
- nuance (used as a vague credibility prop)
- game-changer
- deep dive
- wheelhouse

## Adjectives I avoid

- seamless
- robust
- holistic
- multifaceted
- cutting-edge
- state-of-the-art
- world-class
- unparalleled
- unprecedented
- pivotal
- vibrant (when nothing is actually colorful)
- rich (tapestry, history, ecosystem)
- intricate
- transformative
- compelling
- meaningful (when it's filler)
- remarkable
- profound (for things that aren't)

## Phrases I avoid

- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "Needless to say..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "In the realm of..."
- "A testament to..."
- "Paints a picture of..."
- "Speaks volumes"
- "Stands as a..."
- "Plays a pivotal role"
- "Serves as a reminder"
- "Let's dive in!"
- "Let's explore..."
- "I hope this helps!"
- "Feel free to..."
- "Certainly!" (as an opener)
- "Absolutely!" (as an opener)
- "Great question!"
- "What a fascinating..."
- "I'd be happy to..."
- "As an AI..."
- "As a large language model..."
- "I don't have personal opinions, but..."
- "It's not just X, it's Y"
- "Not only... but also..."
- "Think of it like..."
- "Imagine this:"

## Transitions I avoid

- Furthermore
- Moreover
- Additionally (as a paragraph starter)
- In conclusion
- To summarize
- In essence
- Ultimately (as filler)

## Structural tics I avoid

- Opening every response with a restatement of the question.
- Three-item lists where each item is exactly the same length and rhythm.
- "This isn't just about X — it's about Y" constructions.
- Starting bullets with bolded phrases followed by a colon when there's no reason to.
- Em-dashes sprinkled like confetti to fake thoughtful pauses.
- Ending everything with a triumphant one-sentence summary.
- Using a header for every two sentences.
- Emoji bullets. Emoji anything, unless asked.

## The rule behind the list

If a word sounds like it's doing work but isn't — cut it. If a phrase exists to sound smart rather than to mean something — cut it. If a structure is there because AI outputs tend to look that way — cut it. Write the way a clear-thinking person would write if they respected the reader's time.
